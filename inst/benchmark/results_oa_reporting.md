# Open-access licensing and reporting-guideline validation

Two indicators added in 1.1.0, validated on the **1000-article 2023 open-access
sample**. Every article was read and hand-labeled by the maintainer (the ground
truth), independently of the detector. The validation is **reproducible**: the
row-level human labels are in `data-raw/benchmark/labels_oa_reporting.csv`
(`is_open_access_label`, `oa_license_label`, `is_reporting_label`,
`reporting_guideline_label`) and the metrics are regenerated by
`data-raw/benchmark/run_oa_reporting.R`. 95% confidence intervals are stratified
bootstrap (2000 resamples).

| Indicator | n | Positives | Negatives | Sensitivity | Specificity | PPV | Accuracy |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Open-access license | 1000 | 999 | 1 | 100% | (see note) | 100% | 100% |
| Reporting guideline | 1000 | 65 | 935 | 93.8% [87.7, 98.5] | 99.0% [98.3, 99.6] | 87.1% | 98.7% |

## Open-access licensing

This indicator is **structured metadata extraction**, not a behavioral
classifier: it reads the JATS `<license>` element and its license-reference URL.
The meaningful, well-powered result is the **license-type exact match: 99.8%**
(997/999) against the hand label (CC-BY vs CC-BY-NC / NC-ND / SA, CC0, or
retained copyright).

The binary `is_open_access` is near-deterministic from that structured field. Its
**specificity is not meaningfully estimated here**: the sample is the PMC
open-access subset, so it contains only one non-open (retained-copyright) article
(classified correctly). The "100%" specificity is therefore a ceiling from a
single negative, not a stable estimate; a negative-enriched sample (retained
copyright, hybrid, missing or restricted license) would be needed to estimate it
properly. `rt_accuracy` carries `is_open_access` with this caveat in its `source`
field, and its Rogan-Gladen correction is effectively the identity. Note that
`is_open_access` is **access status**: CC-BY-NC and CC-BY-ND articles are open
access but carry reuse restrictions, which are recorded in `oa_license`.

## Reporting guideline

Detected **precision-first**: a guideline counts only in an author-level
reporting context; common-word acronyms (ARRIVE, CARE, RECORD, REMARK, SPIRIT,
PROCESS) require the upper-case form beside a guideline noun; full spelled-out
names are matched directly; and a veto removes animal-welfare ("Care and Use of
Laboratory Animals"), clinical/treatment, non-adherence ("a PRISMA-compliant
search could not be used"), and **guideline-discourse** mentions (background
descriptions of a guideline, and a systematic review extracting or assessing
whether its *included* studies followed CONSORT/PRISMA/STROBE). The named
guideline is returned. Coverage spans the EQUATOR catalogue (CONSORT, PRISMA and
its extensions, STROBE, ARRIVE, STARD, TRIPOD, COREQ, SRQR, SQUIRE, CHEERS, CARE,
PROCESS, STROCSS, RAMESES) and the wider reportilo guideline list.

The remaining misses (sensitivity 93.8%) are mostly non-English statements and a
few figure captions; the residual false positives (specificity 99.0%) are
guidelines named in a borderline context that the maintainer judged not actually
followed by the article.
